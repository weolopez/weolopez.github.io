About the Randoms

⚽ Who We Are

The Randoms are a Tuesday/Thursday pickup soccer group in Atlanta. No league, no refs, no trophies — just people who love the game showing up twice a week. We've been running for years through heat, rain, and occasionally missing players.

📬 The Email Thread

Coordination is done via a legendary email thread. RSVPs range from the terse ("In") to the elaborate ("In + 47 trumpkins"). Headcounts are manual. This page is an attempt to modernize — slightly.

🚫 The Banned Neighbor

There is, allegedly, a neighbor who was banned from playing. The details are disputed. The ban is not.

🌧️ Rain or Shine

We play in rain. We play in heat. We've played through power outages at nearby fields and through an unfortunate incident involving a goal post and someone's car. The game goes on.

📜 Group History

2015
Peak chaos energy. The Thursday Night Throwdown was in full swing alongside Sunday sessions. Off the pitch: a watch party for the Gold Cup at Mac McGee's in Roswell, a move to Roswell Area Park was coordinated, and someone received a red card in the Orlando Classic finals that the group has not let them forget.
2016
Sunday afternoons at RAP and Thursday evenings at Noonday became the established rhythm. The group ran an internal "Predict the Score" contest for Euro 2016. Good news off the pitch: the Atlanta Silverbacks finally found new owners, celebrated group-wide.
2017
A quiet year in the archive — mostly new players requesting to join the Google Group. The group was growing. Emails were short. Games were played.
2018
Tuesday and Thursday evenings at RAP locked in as core nights. Frequent headcount emails to make sure there were enough bodies to field a game. Rain and field lights were recurring villains.
2019
7:00 PM at RAP. Show up or reply-all your excuse. Field conditions discussed. Games played. The machine kept running.
2020
Started strong — a New Year's Day game, a group photo, a "Happy Random Year" email. Then the records go quiet. The game endured.
2021
Back at RAP and Noonday with regularity. The email thread doubled as a ticket exchange — members offered up and claimed Atlanta United seats between scheduling updates.
2022
Wednesday and Friday sessions at RAP went semi-structured — RSVPs required to cover field rental costs. Noonday remained the chaos option. Highlight: an incident where players split across two different fields and blamed the organizers for being "too hungover to show up." Both sides had a point.
2023
A farewell kick and drinks at Gate City for Chahine Ferrani before his move to Boston. The group also organized an MLK Day kick at RAP. Holiday games became a tradition worth coordinating around.
2024
Thursday Noonday kicks held steady. A March session featured post-game beers, which may or may not have set a precedent. Atlanta United ticket trading continued in the thread alongside the usual headcounts.
2025
Extremely active year. Field availability at Noonday and RAP was a constant battle against youth lacrosse and football. A well-attended Thanksgiving morning kick. Lively debate over Tata Martino returning to coach Atlanta United. And members started posting their luck — or lack thereof — securing 2026 World Cup tickets.
2026 — World Cup Year
The group came roaring back into the archive. Constant field-availability battles across Noonday, Groveway, and RAP — youth leagues, closures, bad turf. Highlights: a well-organized Memorial Day Pre-World Cup Kick at RAP, and the formal permanent ban of a disruptive neighbor, ratified by group consensus.
